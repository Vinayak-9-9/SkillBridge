# SkillBridge

**Bridging the education-employment gap through design thinking**

SkillBridge is a design thinking project by **Group 165** that proposes an integrated system for helping undergraduate students gain, structure, verify, and communicate industry-aligned competencies. The project addresses the gap between academic qualification and workforce readiness through a three-part solution: a **Skill Credits Registry**, a **Live Project Marketplace**, and a **Verified E-Portfolio**.

## Executive Summary

Students spend years in formal education, but many still graduate without the practical exposure, verified proof of ability, or clear role-based guidance needed for modern jobs. Employers face a parallel problem: they see degrees, certificates, and resumes, but often lack trusted, comparable evidence of actual competence.

SkillBridge reframes this as a structural readiness gap rather than an individual failure. The proposed system creates a parallel credentialing layer on top of existing degrees. It lets students convert external learning and live project work into recognized skill credits, gain practical exposure through short industry projects, and present verified project evidence through an employer-readable portfolio.

## Domain Analysis: Education and Employability Gap

The project starts from a systemic disconnect between education output and job readiness.

### Critical Gap

- Only about **5%** of India's workforce is formally skilled.
- Source used in the project: World Economic Forum, 2022.

### Current Status

- Graduate employability rate is listed as **54.8%** for 2025.
- Source used in the project: India Skills Report, 2025.

### Readiness Leak Pipeline

The presentation frames employability as a pipeline where students gradually fall out before becoming job-ready:

1. Total graduates
2. Academically qualified students
3. Students with soft skills
4. Students with industry exposure
5. Job-ready and skilled talent

The central insight is that while more than half of graduates may be employable by potential, only a small fraction have formal, verified, industry-recognized skills. The result is a high volume of graduates but a low volume of job-ready talent.

## Problem Statement

Students invest years in education but remain systematically underprepared for the modern workforce.

The problem is not only what is taught. It is also how skills are validated, trusted, and signaled to the market.

### Root Cause 1: Exam-Centric System

Curricula often prioritize rote memorization, theoretical grades, and classroom performance over practical application.

Key issues:

- Theory-heavy assessments
- Outdated syllabi
- Misaligned soft skills

Project source signal: Cengage 2025 mismatch findings.

### Root Cause 2: Limited Industry Exposure

Students often lack real-world context. Internships become a decisive hiring factor, but access to internships is unequal.

Key issues:

- No live project experience
- Classroom isolation
- Low professional awareness

Project source signal: NACE Job Outlook 2026.

### Root Cause 3: Fragmented Signaling

Employers cannot reliably verify competence beyond degrees. Self-learning, online courses, and informal projects often remain unrecognized or untrusted.

Key issues:

- Diplomas do not equal competence
- Self-learning is invisible
- Hiring has a trust deficit

Project source signal: World Bank 2025.

### Outcome: Systemic Underpreparedness

The outcome is a generation of graduates who are degree-qualified but work-unready. This creates an efficiency loss for students, employers, institutions, and the wider economy.

Impact:

- High unemployment among educated youth
- Talent shortages for employers

## User Research Synthesis

The project synthesizes findings from four key studies spanning 2022-2026.

### Insight 1: The Confidence Crisis

Nearly half of recent graduates report feeling unprepared to even apply for entry-level roles.

Design implication:

- Students do not only need content.
- They need clarity, proof, confidence, and a structured path toward employability.

Project source: Cengage Graduate Employability Report, 2025.

### Insight 2: Experience Matters More Than Grades

Internships are presented as the number one deciding factor for employers choosing between equally qualified candidates.

Influence indicators shown in the project:

| Factor | Influence |
| --- | --- |
| Internship experience | 95% |
| College major | 65% |
| GPA above 3.0 | 40% |

Design implication:

- Work experience cannot remain optional or restricted to students who win scarce internships.
- Live projects and micro-internships should broaden access to experience.

Project source: NACE Job Outlook, 2026.

### Insight 3: The Expectation Mismatch

Employers demand job-specific hard skills, while educators often focus heavily on broad soft skills.

Design implication:

- Students need role-specific pathways.
- The system should translate market demand into clear skill-building routes.

Project source: Cengage Group, 2025.

### Insight 4: India's Certification Void

India shows high general employability potential but lacks formal, verified skill credentials at scale.

Indicators shown in the project:

- **54.8%** employability potential
- Only **5%** formally skilled

Design implication:

- The system must recognize non-formal learning, external courses, and real projects through trusted credits and verification.

Project sources: World Economic Forum 2022 and India Skills Report 2025.

## Design, UI, and UX

SkillBridge is designed as a clean, trust-first digital ecosystem rather than a noisy job portal or generic learning platform. The user experience should make students feel guided, employers feel confident, and institutions feel that the system can fit into existing academic structures without heavy disruption.

### Design Principles

| Principle | Meaning in SkillBridge |
| --- | --- |
| Clarity over overload | Students should immediately understand what skills they have, what they lack, and what to do next. |
| Proof over claims | The interface should prioritize verified projects, skill credits, artifacts, and evaluator feedback instead of vague profile statements. |
| Guidance over browsing | The product should recommend role-based pathways and live projects rather than forcing students to search blindly. |
| Trust by design | Verification status, credential sources, consent controls, and employer access should be visible and easy to understand. |
| Low-friction adoption | Institutions should be able to pilot the system with limited curriculum changes. |

### Primary Users

SkillBridge serves three main user groups:

- **Students:** Build job readiness through skill credits, live projects, verified portfolios, and role-based guidance.
- **Institutions:** Recognize external learning, embed experiential credits, and track student readiness outcomes.
- **Employers:** Discover candidates through verified evidence of competence, not only degrees or resumes.

### Student Experience

The student journey should feel like a guided readiness dashboard.

Core student screens and interactions:

- **Readiness Dashboard:** Shows workforce readiness score, completed credits, active projects, missing skills, and recommended next steps.
- **Skill Pathways:** Role-based paths such as frontend developer, data analyst, product designer, or business analyst, with required skills mapped to credits and projects.
- **Skill Credits Registry:** Displays earned credits from MOOCs, certifications, workshops, and verified external learning.
- **Live Project Marketplace:** Lets students browse short 2-4 week projects filtered by role, difficulty, skill area, mentor, and deadline.
- **Project Workspace:** Provides deliverables, submission checkpoints, mentor feedback, rubric-based evaluation, and completion status.
- **Verified E-Portfolio:** Collects project artifacts such as GitHub links, reports, designs, certificates, badges, and evaluator comments into an employer-readable profile.

The UX goal is to reduce student anxiety by replacing vague advice with visible progress and concrete actions.

### Employer Experience

Employers should be able to evaluate candidates quickly through trusted proof.

Core employer screens and interactions:

- **Candidate Discovery:** Filter students by skill credits, verified badges, project category, tools used, and readiness level.
- **Portfolio Review:** View project artifacts, evaluator notes, rubrics, and verification status in one place.
- **Skill Badge Verification:** Check whether a badge or credit was earned through a verified project, certification, or audited assessment.
- **Project Posting:** Create live project briefs with scope, required skills, duration, deliverables, and evaluation criteria.

The UX goal is to reduce hiring uncertainty by making competence visible, comparable, and verifiable.

### Institution Experience

Institutions need a management layer that makes adoption practical.

Core institution screens and interactions:

- **Pilot Dashboard:** Track student participation, active projects, credits issued, and placement-related indicators.
- **Credit Approval Workflow:** Review external learning submissions and approve them against NEP 2020 or AICTE-aligned criteria.
- **Rubric Management:** Standardize how projects and skill demonstrations are evaluated.
- **Partner Management:** Manage employer partners, mentors, certifiers, and audit partners.

The UX goal is to make experiential learning manageable at scale without requiring immediate full curriculum reform.

### Information Architecture

The product can be organized around five major areas:

1. **Dashboard:** Personalized readiness overview and next actions.
2. **Pathways:** Role-based learning and project roadmaps.
3. **Projects:** Live project marketplace, submissions, and feedback.
4. **Credits:** Skill credit registry, verification, and approval status.
5. **Portfolio:** Public or consent-based profile for employers.

### Visual Design Direction

The visual language should communicate credibility, academic seriousness, and modern employability.

Recommended UI direction:

- Clean dashboard layout with strong hierarchy
- Card-based modules for credits, projects, badges, and portfolio artifacts
- Progress bars for readiness scores and pathway completion
- Status chips for verified, pending, rejected, and audited items
- Simple icons for credits, projects, portfolios, institutions, and employers
- Tables and filters for employer and institution workflows
- Calm professional colors, using blue for progress/trust, green for completion, purple for verified credentials, and red only for risks or blockers

The design should avoid looking like a social media feed. The focus should stay on structured progress, evidence, and employability outcomes.

### Trust and Consent UX

Because SkillBridge handles student performance data, privacy and consent are central to the experience.

Important trust features:

- Students control which portfolio items are visible to employers.
- Every credential shows its source, evaluator, date, and verification status.
- Employers see verification metadata, not private student data by default.
- Institutions can audit approvals and project evaluations.
- Consent-based sharing supports compliance with India's DPDP Act.

### Accessibility and Usability

The interface should be simple enough for students from different college tiers and backgrounds.

Usability priorities:

- Plain language labels instead of policy-heavy jargon
- Mobile-friendly dashboards for students
- Fast filtering and comparison tools for employers
- Clear empty states that tell users what action to take next
- Visible progress indicators to encourage completion
- Simple onboarding that asks for career goals, current skills, and available proof

## Final Solution: The SkillBridge System

### Module 01: Skill Credits Registry

Purpose: Academic integration.

The Skill Credits Registry recognizes credible external learning and converts it into structured academic or employability value.

Features:

- Awards academic credits for external MOOCs and certifications
- Aligns with the National Credit Framework under NEP 2020
- Standardizes non-formal learning hours
- Creates mobility across learning sources

Research-to-feature logic:

- Research insight: High employability potential is masked by lack of formal skilling and need for credit mobility.
- Design response: Skill Credits Registry lets students "cash in" external effort for degree progress, incentivizing upskilling.

### Module 02: Live Project Marketplace

Purpose: Industry exposure.

The Live Project Marketplace gives students access to short, scoped, real-world projects.

Features:

- Short-term micro-internships lasting 2-4 weeks
- Projects sourced with industry mentors
- Real-world problem statements instead of purely theoretical assignments
- Scoped deliverables evaluated by professionals
- Broader access to experience beyond competitive summer internships

Research-to-feature logic:

- Research insight: Internships and work experience are deciding factors for employers.
- Design response: The marketplace democratizes access to practical experience.

### Module 03: Verified E-Portfolio

Purpose: Signaling mechanism.

The Verified E-Portfolio turns student work into trusted, employer-readable proof.

Features:

- Dynamic repository of project artifacts such as code, designs, and reports
- Employer-readable badges of competence
- Trust layer through blockchain verification
- Consent-based evidence sharing with employers

Research-to-feature logic:

- Research insight: Digital credentials and verified portfolios support the shift from degree-only hiring to portfolio-based hiring.
- Design response: Verified E-Portfolio gives employers stronger evidence of competence than a resume alone.

## Implementation Strategy

The project identifies four main implementation risks and proposes corresponding mitigation strategies.

### Challenge 1: Institutional Resistance

Barrier level: High.

Problem:

Universities may hesitate to recognize external credits because of rigid curriculum structures and accreditation concerns.

Mitigation:

- Align with NEP 2020 and AICTE frameworks
- Use existing government mandates for credit flexibility
- Begin with pilot memorandums of understanding with autonomous colleges
- Demonstrate success through pilots before wider rollout

### Challenge 2: Validity of Assessments

Barrier level: Medium risk.

Problem:

Employers may doubt the rigor of live project evaluations compared with standardized tests or grades.

Mitigation:

- Use standardized rubrics for project evaluation
- Add proctored tasks where needed
- Conduct random third-party audits
- Partner with industry-recognized certifiers to validate outcomes

### Challenge 3: Scalability and Operations

Barrier level: Operational.

Problem:

Manually sourcing and verifying thousands of live projects for millions of students is not scalable.

Mitigation:

- Build a self-service project marketplace
- Use automated code checks
- Use peer review for initial filtering
- Phase rollout and verification to reduce manual load

### Challenge 4: Data Privacy and Trust

Barrier level: Compliance.

Problem:

Student performance data and employer access raise privacy and misuse concerns.

Mitigation:

- Use consent-based sharing protocols
- Give students control over who views their data
- Use secure credential wallets
- Explore blockchain-enabled credential verification
- Maintain compliance with India's DPDP Act

## Proposed Survey Instrument

Target audience: undergraduate students in years 2-4.

### Theme: Readiness and Exposure

1. On a scale of 1-5, how prepared do you feel to apply for entry-level jobs in your field today?
   - Scale: 1 = not prepared, 5 = very prepared.

2. Which area do you feel is your biggest gap regarding employability?
   - Options: technical skills, soft skills, industry knowledge, practical experience.

3. Do you currently possess verifiable evidence of your skills, such as a portfolio, live project links, or GitHub?
   - Options: yes, no, partial.

4. Have you completed a formal internship relevant to your field of study?
   - Options: yes, no.

5. What is the primary barrier preventing you from securing internships?
   - Options: lack of openings, skill mismatch, no connections, schedule conflict.

### Theme: Learning Habits and Solution Fit

6. Where do you primarily acquire job-relevant skills?
   - Rank: college curriculum, self-learning through YouTube/blogs, paid courses, peer projects.

7. If you could receive academic credits for completing verifiable external projects, how interested would you be?
   - Scale: 1 = not interested, 5 = extremely interested.

8. Which format of industry exposure do you prefer?
   - Options: traditional internship of 2 months, or micro-live projects of 2-4 weeks.

9. Would you consent to a system where employers can directly verify your project portfolio through a secure platform?
   - Options: yes, no, unsure.

### Demographic Data Collected

- Year of study
- Stream or major
- College tier

## Core Takeaway

SkillBridge is designed to solve the readiness gap by making student effort visible, structured, and trusted. It does not replace degrees; it strengthens them with verified proof of practical competence. The project's central promise is a bridge between learning and employment: students gain clearer pathways and credible evidence, institutions gain an agile mechanism for experiential learning, and employers gain stronger signals for hiring.
